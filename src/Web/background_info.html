Project Background Information

"BECAUSE EVERY BREATH MATTERS."
Monitoring air quality through smart technology.

Overview

This project aims to monitor air quality using a custom-built sensor system that collects environmental data and displays it through a web dashboard. The system measures important air quality indicators such as particulate matter, temperature, humidity, and gas concentrations. These readings are processed and presented in a simple and accessible interface so users can easily understand the current environmental conditions. By providing real-time data and visual insights, the system helps raise awareness about air pollution and encourages informed decisions to promote healthier environments.

Why Air Quality Matters?

According to the World Health Organisation, air pollution is one of the greatest environmental risks to health.
By reducing air pollution levels, boroughs can reduce the burden of disease from stroke, heart disease, lung cancer, and respiratory diseases including asthma.
Studies show air pollution can reduce life expectancy by 7 to 8 months on average. WHO promotes interventions and initiatives for healthy sectoral policies (including energy, transport, housing, urban development and electrification of health-care facilities), addressing key risks to health from air pollution indoors and outdoors, and contributing to achieving health co-benefits from climate change mitigation policies.

So How Does The Sensor Work?

Our system uses sensors such as Gas and BME680 sensors to measure particulate matter and gas concentrations. It uses a combination of particulate matter sensors, gas sensors, and the BME680 environmental sensor to provide a detailed and accurate assessment of surrounding air conditions. The particulate matter sensor measures PM1.0, PM2.5, and PM10 levels to detect fine particles from sources such as dust, smoke, and vehicle emissions. Gas sensors complement this by identifying harmful pollutants like carbon monoxide, nitrogen dioxide, and volatile organic compounds released from household products and outdoor pollution. The BME680 adds further insight by measuring temperature, humidity, pressure, and gas resistance, which helps estimate overall indoor air quality. By integrating data from all these sensors, the system delivers real‑time, reliable information that helps users understand pollution levels, identify potential health risks, and make informed decisions to improve their environment.

Data Flow

Sensor collects readings → sends them to server → stores them in database → displays them on the dashboard.
Our system follows a clear end‑to‑end data flow that ensures air‑quality readings are captured, processed, and presented reliably. The sensors continuously collect environmental measurements, such as particulate matter levels, gas concentrations, temperature, and humidity. These raw readings are then transmitted to the server, where they are validated, formatted, and prepared for storage. Once received, the server writes the data into a structured database, allowing it to be organized chronologically and retrieved efficiently for analysis. From there, the dashboard accesses the stored information and visualizes it through graphs, charts, and real‑time indicators, giving users an intuitive and accessible way to monitor air‑quality trends and understand changes in their environment.

Team Roles

  • Data Science: Responsible for analyzing the collected data and providing insights into air quality trends and patterns.

Real-World Applications

Air quality monitoring plays a vital role in protecting public health and creating healthier environments across schools, cities, workplaces, and research settings. By continuously tracking pollutants such as particulate matter, harmful gases, and volatile organic compounds, these systems help identify pollution sources and reveal patterns that might otherwise go unnoticed. The data collected empowers communities and decision‑makers to take meaningful action—whether that means improving ventilation in classrooms, enforcing cleaner transportation policies in urban areas, or guiding workplace safety measures. Over time, consistent monitoring not only raises awareness but also drives long‑term change by supporting cleaner technologies, informing public health initiatives, and encouraging individuals and organizations to adopt more sustainable practices.
